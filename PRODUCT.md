# Product

## Register

product

## Users

KeyPilot serves users who manage many website accounts inside the browser: site owners, foreign trade operators, affiliate marketers, ad buyers, developers, and business operations teams. They move between admin panels and need fast login without putting passwords in spreadsheets, chat apps, or remote services.

## Product Purpose

KeyPilot is a local-first Chrome and Edge password manager and auto-login extension. It stores credentials in an encrypted local Vault, unlocks with a master password, supports saving, importing, filling, generating, and one-click login workflows, and keeps website/account data on the user's machine.

## Brand Personality

Secure, clear, professional, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a serious browser tool for daily operational work, closer to RoboForm, 1Password, Bitwarden, and modern SaaS admin surfaces than to a decorative mobile mini-app.

## Anti-references

Do not make the interface look like a generic mobile app, WeChat mini program, cartoon product, traditional antivirus suite, cyberpunk terminal, heavy dark hacker tool, purple concept UI, or a screen centered around a large floating circular button.

## Design Principles

1. Security is visible but calm: sensitive actions explain risk without creating panic.
2. The current browser task comes first: popup screens prioritize current-site matching, credential actions, and quick recovery.
3. Local-first should be legible: encryption and no-upload behavior are clear in copy and states.
4. Professional density beats decoration: compact layouts, clear hierarchy, and predictable controls matter more than marketing visuals.
5. Dangerous actions are deliberate: reset, delete, reveal, and copy flows should be explicit and reversible where possible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard access for forms and primary actions, visible focus states, readable Chinese UI copy, reduced-motion friendly transitions, and no reliance on color alone for security or error states.
